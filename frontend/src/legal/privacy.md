# Privacy Policy

**Last updated:** June 2026

Well Done ("the app", "we", "us") is built by Joey Fadul. This policy explains what data the app collects, how it's used, and the choices you have. The intent is for it to be short and honest. If anything here is unclear, email **joefadul@gmail.com**.

---

## 1. Who this app is for

Well Done is a tool for **teachers** to track student behavior and reward progress within their own classroom. Accounts in the app are for adults. Teachers are responsible for following their school's and district's policies before recording any information about a student.

## 2. What we collect

### Information you provide
- **Email and password** when you create an account. We use Amazon Cognito to handle authentication. Your password is hashed by Cognito and never stored or visible to us.
- **Classroom and student information** that you enter — classroom names, student names, grade levels, profile photos, behavior notes, and the points/rewards you grant or revoke.

### Information generated by your use of the app
- A log of every reward action (timestamp, point change, reason text) so the app can show activity history and undo recent changes.
- A token issued by Cognito to keep you signed in. On iOS this token is stored in the device's **Keychain**; on the web it is stored in your browser's localStorage.

### What we don't collect
- We don't use analytics SDKs (no Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Firebase).
- We don't use advertising or tracking identifiers.
- We don't access your location, contacts, calendar, or microphone.
- We don't access your photos beyond what you explicitly choose to upload.

## 3. How we use it

Only to make the app work for you:
- Sign you in and keep you signed in.
- Show your classrooms, students, points, and activity history on your devices.
- Generate short-lived URLs so you can view photos you've uploaded.
- Email you a 6-digit verification code at sign-up.

We do not use your data for advertising. We do not sell it. We do not share it with third-party marketers.

## 4. Where it lives

Everything is hosted on **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** in the United States (us-east-1):
- Authentication: AWS Cognito
- Application database: AWS DynamoDB
- Photos: AWS S3 (private bucket; photos are only retrievable through short-lived signed URLs the app generates on your behalf)
- Application code: AWS Lambda + API Gateway

All traffic between the app and our servers is encrypted in transit (HTTPS / TLS 1.2+).

## 5. How long we keep it

- **While your account is active**, we keep everything you've entered so the app remains usable.
- **When you delete a student**, the student is removed from the active classroom but their record is retained in archived school years so historical reports stay accurate.
- **When you delete a classroom**, all of its content is removed permanently from our database.
- **When you delete your account** from Settings → Delete Account, every classroom you own is permanently removed (including its students, photos, and activity history). For any classroom you joined as a member but don't own, you are removed from it (the classroom and its owner's data are untouched). Your authentication record is then permanently removed.

## 6. Your choices

- You can edit or delete any classroom, student, or activity record at any time from inside the app.
- You can delete your account directly from the app: **Settings → Delete Account**.
- You can request a copy of your data by emailing the same address.

## 7. Children's information

Well Done is **not directed at children**. Accounts are intended for educators. Information *about* children (such as a student's name or photo) may be entered by a teacher as part of running their classroom. We rely on the teacher to have obtained any consent required by their school, district, or applicable law (including FERPA in the U.S.).

If you believe a child's information has been entered into Well Done in a way that violates the law, please email **joefadul@gmail.com** and we will remove it.

## 8. Security

- Passwords must be at least 12 characters and include uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
- Authentication tokens are stored in the iOS Keychain on native devices.
- Photo uploads and reads go through short-lived presigned URLs; the storage bucket itself is private.
- The web frontend is protected by AWS WAF (rate limits and known-bad-input filters).
- Backups are kept via DynamoDB Point-in-Time Recovery.

## 9. Third parties

The only third party that processes data on our behalf is **Amazon Web Services**, which operates as our infrastructure provider. AWS's privacy practices are documented at <https://aws.amazon.com/privacy/>.

We do not use third-party advertising, analytics, A/B testing, crash reporting, or customer support tools.

## 10. Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we will change the "Last updated" date at the top. Material changes will be announced in the app before they take effect.
